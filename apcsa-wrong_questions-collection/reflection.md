### What changed compared to my plan

In `plan.md` I said I would log mistakes the same day as each practice test. For Barron Online **FRQ Set 3**, I actually added the three rows to `frq.md` two days later because I rushed to the next topic first. I also planned to fill `mcq.md` after every full test, but I skipped one session and had to rely on memory for two questions—which made my *Why I got it wrong* notes vaguer than I wanted.

### What was difficult

The hardest part was being **specific** in the *Why I lost points* column for FRQs. For **Q1**, I knew I messed up the loop bound, but I first wrote “off-by-one error” without saying I used `<= arr.length`. Naming the exact bug took an extra minute but made the row useful later. For MCQs, I kept choosing answers based on **vibes** on inheritance questions instead of tracing types line by line; writing the *Correct rule / fix* forced me to state the rule in plain language.

### What I learned

I learned that **one row per mistake** in the right file (`mcq.md` vs `frq.md`) saves time before the exam: I can sort by *Reminder* and drill only those phrases. I also learned that **past paper** rows are the most valuable when *Source* includes **year and form**, because I can pull the original PDF and rubric without guessing which test I meant.
